Adaptablity & Stress Management
How do handle stressful situation?
S.T.A.R
Situation:
During a critical ETL pipeline deployment in Airflow, we encountered a major issue where the scheduled jobs were consistently failing to start on time, causing significant delays in data processing. This was especially stressful as it was the end of the quarter, and the data was needed for business reports that would drive important strategic decisions. The delays caused panic within the team, as it threatened to impact decision-making, and the pressure was mounting to resolve the issue quickly.

Task:
My task was to quickly investigate and fix the issue, ensuring that the jobs triggered on time to prevent further delays in reporting, while managing the increased stress and tight deadlines.

Action:
I remained calm and methodical, immediately diving into the Airflow logs and identifying that the issue stemmed from an incorrect start_date and misconfigured catchup settings, which caused the jobs to be misaligned with the schedule. With time running out, I corrected the configurations, disabled the catchup setting, and optimized task dependencies to prevent cascading delays. I also implemented custom alerts to monitor future delays and added retry logic to ensure job execution stability.

Result:
The issue was resolved under pressure, and the ETL pipeline resumed running on schedule. The reports were delivered on time, allowing the business to make critical decisions without further disruption. The proactive alerting system and optimization of the pipeline ensured smoother operation in the future, helping the team manage stress better in subsequent projects.
